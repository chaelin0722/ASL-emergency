    Signing For Care

    AI-Powered ASL Learning for Emergency Medical Responders

    Empowering responders to communicate clearly with Deaf and Hard of Hearing patients through accessible, real-time learning tools.

    Scroll to Learn More
    “It’s frustrating when patients need help and you can’t communicate efficiently.”
    — Participant, Tulane EMS

    Project Statement

    • "Signing for Care” is an AI-powered American Sign Language (ASL) learning platform designed to bridge communication gaps between Emergency Medical Responders (EMRs) and Deaf or Hard of Hearing (DHH) patients.
    • Built at Tulane University’s A11Y Lab , the project combines accessibility research, human-computer interaction, and applied machine learning to promote equity in healthcare access.

    Key Statistics

    1. 83% of EMRs reported losing critical information when communicating with Deaf patients
    2. Paramedics frequently report struggling to obtain complete medical histories
    3. EMRs show strong receptiveness to communication training and technology; for instance, all 148 participants in a communication training study found it helpful even after three months
